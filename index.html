About

Focus: reconstructing the real rather than generating the plausible

The Workshop on Generative 3D Reconstruction focuses on bridging generative modeling and geometric reconstruction to recover real-world 3D structure from incomplete observations. Unlike approaches emphasizing visual plausibility, GenRecon3D targets faithful recovery of underlying geometry using learned priors, uncertainty-aware reasoning, and physically grounded models. The workshop unites researchers across neural scene representations, implicit geometry, and 3D Gaussian methods, with the goal of establishing principles and metrics for accurate, trustworthy, and generalizable reconstruction. The program will feature invited talks, oral presentations, poster sessions, and discussions shaping the next generation of generative 3D vision methods.

Broader impacts and ethics

Trustworthy reconstruction and responsible deployment

The workshop advances generative reconstruction methods that target truthful recovery of real environments, with relevance to robotics, autonomous systems, digital twins, and AR/VR. The program highlights evaluation protocols for geometric and semantic accuracy, robustness under partial observations, and uncertainty quantification.

Social and ethical considerations

We will screen submissions for ethical misconduct and misuse, encourage work on transparency and bias mitigation, and include an ethics-focused segment within the panel discussion.
